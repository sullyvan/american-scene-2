The Bridging Model

Unlike engagement algorithms that optimize within communities, bridging-based ranking rewards content that resonates across different groups. A work that appeals only to German Expressionists scores low. One that speaks to photographers, muralists, and printmakers alike scores high.

The Agent Network

20 LACMA collection artists and 14 LACMA curators serve as resonance agents — each with a keyword profile drawn from their expertise, their department, and their known critical sensibility. The bridging score is their collective response.

The Scale Argument

At 50,000 nodes, a high bridging score means the work reaches across zip codes, languages, and art traditions simultaneously. That is what a Federal Art Project does. That is what an engagement algorithm cannot do by design.
