One-Page Overview

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & RFP DIRECTIVE
RFP DIRECTIVE — 150 WORDS

The American Scene 2.0 seeks artists to create computational works documenting contemporary American life through machine intelligence. We commission projects that critically examine algorithmic systems shaping society: labor, surveillance, infrastructure, community. Successful proposals demonstrate technical rigor, aesthetic innovation, and public engagement. Artists receive Lab access, technical mentorship, fabrication budget, exhibition platform. We prioritize projects interrogating AI's role in cultural production while honoring WPA's legacy of democratic art access. Deliverables include open-source code, public documentation, physical/digital exhibition. Selection considers artistic merit, feasibility, community impact. This is not tech demonstration—it is cultural critique through computational means. We fund artists building better questions, not just better tools. Apply through LACMA Art + Technology Lab.

WORD COUNT: 150
MISSION

Bridge 1930s Federal Art Project ethos with contemporary AI systems. Democratize access to computational tools for cultural documentation.

SCOPE

12-month residencies. $70K production budgets. Cross-disciplinary mentorship. Public exhibition at LACMA. Open-source mandate required.

IMPACT

Archive American life for future historians. Train next generation of civic technologists. Prove art institutions lead AI discourse.
